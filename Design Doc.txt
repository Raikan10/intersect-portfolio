
Portfolio Design System

Computational Geometry × Architectural Lighting

Concept

The portfolio should feel less like a traditional website and more like a digital installation or architectural space. The design language emphasizes structure, geometry, light, and silence.

The core themes are:
	•	systems thinking
	•	intersections of ideas
	•	structure and precision
	•	minimal but intentional design
	•	spatial navigation rather than menu-based browsing

The experience should evoke:
	•	entering a gallery
	•	interacting with a constructed system
	•	watching ideas form

⸻

Core Symbol

The central mark is a radial geometric intersection structure composed of thin vector lines radiating from a central node.

Conceptually the symbol represents:
	•	intersections
	•	decision points
	•	systems
	•	vectors
	•	networks

It functions as the structural DNA of the website.

Possible interpretations:
	•	radial geometric structure
	•	vector constellation
	•	parametric star
	•	structural frame

⸻

Navigation Concept

Instead of traditional navigation menus, the symbol can function as a spatial navigation system.

Example structure:

        projects
           ↑
ideas ←  INTERSECT  → writing
           ↓
         about

Each node corresponds to a section of the site.

Hovering or clicking nodes reveals sections.

⸻

Design Language

Computational Geometry

Visual elements are based on:
	•	thin vector lines
	•	nodes and connections
	•	angular geometry
	•	structural symmetry

Motifs to repeat throughout the site:

Element	Meaning
nodes	ideas
lines	connections
intersections	insights
angles	direction
constellations	systems


⸻

Architectural Lighting

Instead of heavy color usage, the site relies on light and contrast.

Visual approach:
	•	dark environment
	•	geometry revealed by light
	•	subtle glow along edges
	•	soft gradient illumination

Influence from architectural lighting installations.

⸻

Color Palette

Primary palette:

Near Black: #040302
Soft White: #F7F4EE
Warm Taupe: #94806D
Muted Bronze: #A48A72

There should be no cool accent color in the hero.
The visual system should stay within black, brown, bronze, and warm off-white.
Any emphasis should come from contrast, opacity, and scale rather than a bright accent.

⸻

Motion Design

Motion should feel physical and deliberate, like kinetic sculpture.

Rules:
	•	slow
	•	smooth
	•	subtle
	•	precise

Possible interactions:

Interaction	Effect
page load	lines draw from center
hover node	connections illuminate
scroll	geometry rotates slightly
section change	network redraws

The system should feel alive but calm.

⸻

Typography System

Typography should feel architectural and editorial.

Recommended typefaces:
	•	Söhne
	•	Neue Haas Grotesk
	•	IBM Plex Sans
	•	Inter

Typography principles:
	•	large headings
	•	generous whitespace
	•	minimal decoration
	•	strong hierarchy

Example hero layout:

AJAY RAJNIKANTH

systems thinker
product builder
ai × strategy


⸻

Layout System

The geometry of the symbol can generate the grid system for the site.

Possible applications:
	•	section divisions aligned with angles
	•	diagonal visual separators
	•	geometric cropping of images
	•	line-based layout structures

This ensures visual consistency across the site.

⸻

Hero Section Concept

Initial page experience:
	1.	A near-black screen with a restrained warm brown lift toward the bottom.
	2.	Subtle grain gives the surface texture without becoming noisy.
	3.	The geometric symbol sits within the atmosphere rather than generating the background.
	4.	Title and navigation appear in warm off-white.

Example:

AJAY RAJNIKANTH

Subtitle:

strategy × technology × systems

The effect should feel quiet, minimal, and cinematic rather than generative or theatrical.

Hero background rules:
	•	top of screen should stay almost fully black
	•	bottom can carry a soft brown gradient rise
	•	grain should be fine and understated
	•	no red, blue, or neon glow
	•	grid lines, if present, should be extremely faint and warm-toned

⸻

Visual Motifs

The following elements should appear repeatedly across the site:
	•	thin vector lines
	•	node points
	•	intersections
	•	geometric angles
	•	constellation structures

This repetition creates a coherent visual language.

⸻

Surreal Element

To prevent the design from feeling sterile, subtle surreal motion can be introduced.

Examples:
	•	nodes slightly floating
	•	connections behaving like tensioned strings
	•	geometry responding gently to cursor movement
	•	faint pulsing light at intersections

These effects should remain almost imperceptible.

⸻

Advanced Option: Generative Geometry

The symbol can be implemented using WebGL or generative graphics.

Benefits:
	•	interactive structure
	•	dynamic line behavior
	•	subtle physics-based movement
	•	deeper spatial feeling

This approach can elevate the portfolio into a premium design experience.

⸻

Desired Emotional Tone

Visitors should feel:
	•	curiosity
	•	calm focus
	•	intellectual clarity
	•	sense of exploration

The portfolio should feel like entering a space of ideas, not browsing a résumé.

⸻

Summary

The design system is built around:
	1.	a central geometric intersection symbol
	2.	computational geometry as visual language
	3.	architectural lighting for depth and atmosphere
	4.	minimal typography and whitespace
	5.	subtle motion and generative structure

The result should feel like a digital installation about systems and ideas.
